The five files
    What is a Brand Brain

    Your brand's official context, in one place

    A framework for consistent, efficient AI use across our brands.

    The Brand Brain is your brand's official context, in one place. Every time someone on your team uses AI, it needs to understand who we are. Without a shared source of truth, every person recreates that context differently. The Brand Brain solves that with a single, maintained file everyone points to.

    The core idea
    Without it

    Each person writes their own context prompt. Inconsistent, inefficient, and off-brand more often than not.

    With a Brand Brain

    One official context file. Everyone points to it. The AI always starts from the same foundation.

    The result

    Consistent AI output. Faster workflows. "On-brand" by default, every time.

    What it is not
    MisconceptionReality
    A complex AI systemJust a text file. No special software required.
    Permanent AI trainingRead fresh at the start of each session, not baked into the model.
    A one-time setupA living document, maintained and updated as the brand evolves.
    Each person's responsibilityOwned centrally by the brand or CoE, referenced by everyone.

    A simple text file that lives in a shared folder. The sections cover who our brand is and how we communicate. Any AI you use reads the Brand Brain context at the start of each session. The more complete and accurate the file, the better the AI will represent your brand.

    Sections in the Brand Brain
    brand-profile

    Who you are, what you sell, your positioning, your competitors, and what you're not. The foundation every other file refers to.

    style-guide

    Voice, tone, terminology, and formatting: the rules that make output sound like your brand, not a generic AI response.

    audience

    Who you're for, what they actually want, their objections, and the exact language they use.

    keyword-map

    The topics, questions, and search terms you want to own and be found for.

    never-do

    The hard rules: banned claims, off-limits positioning, and AI operating instructions.

    What a section looks like (example)
    ## Brand voice - Exodus Adventure Travels We are expert, warm and grounded. We've led small-group adventures for 50 years and it shows. We speak to active, curious travellers who want to see a place properly - on foot, by bike, with a local leader who knows it inside out. ## Do not say - "Bucket list" - undermines the depth of our trips - "Tailor-made" - we run expertly-planned group adventures - "World-class" - vague; show it, don't claim it - Any "#1" claim without a source

    The files live in a shared location anyone on the team can access. One central location. Each brand has its own set of files. The team links to them, agents are trained on them, and when the files are updated, everyone benefits automatically, no re-briefing required.

    /brand-brain/ le-boat-brand-profile.md le-boat-style-guide.md le-boat-audience.md le-boat-keyword-map.md le-boat-never-do.md

    One Brand Brain, many components. Each brand can have as many components as it needs, creative, sales, SEO, acquisition, and more. Anything that needs to stay consistent and is regularly added as AI context should be captured and standardised in the Brand Brain.

    Two ways to use it, day-to-day prompting and agent builds. The least efficient is to paste the context into a new prompt; the more efficient is to link your prompt to the shared file, so it always reads the latest version.

    Day-to-day: how a session starts

    Understood. I've read the Exodus Adventure Travel brand brain. I have your positioning, voice guidelines, competitor context, and do/don't list. What would you like to work on?

    Three options, staying in your direct, expert voice, no "bucket list" language:
    1. Morocco in August: what to expect on the ground

    "Before we begin, please read this context about our brand: [paste or link the file]" Then ask your actual question. That's the entire workflow.

    Output quality: same brief, different result
    Without a Brand Brain

    "We are a premium adventure travel company that offers tailor-made, world-class experiences to discerning bucket-list travellers seeking unforgettable journeys..."

    Generic. Off-voice. Uses three phrases we explicitly banned.
    With a Brand Brain

    "Exodus runs small-group trips that go deeper than the itinerary. Our guides aren't just local, they're specialists. We work with people who've already seen the highlights and want to understand the place."

    On-brand. On-voice. No banned phrases.

    Two problems solved: consistency and efficiency. Right now, every person who uses AI starts from a different place. The Brand Brain makes the starting line the same for everyone, and gets them to the actual work faster.

    Consistency

    Without consistent, shared context, one person's prompt may cover tone, while another's covers competitors, while another covers neither, and the AI gives three versions of your brand. With a Brand Brain, the AI's understanding is identical across every session and every user, because they're all reading the same file.

    Efficiency

    You stop writing setup paragraphs before every prompt. You stop correcting the AI when it uses the wrong tone or off-limits phrasing. You point to the file, then ask your question. The context work is done once, centrally, by the people who know the brand best.

    The bigger picture

    Your Brand Brain becomes your institutional memory. As AI becomes a standard tool across our teams, it's the memory your AI operates from. Instead of each person training the AI through trial and error, you build that knowledge once, maintain it centrally, and everyone benefits.

    For a portfolio like Travelopia, this compounds. Each of our brands has its own positioning, competitors, and guardrails, and the Brand Brain is what keeps each of them sounding like themselves, even as the AI tooling and the people using it change over time.

    Next steps

    You've exported your files - now give your Brand Brain a home

    Your Brand Brain is five Markdown files. To make it your team's single source of truth, store it where everyone can reach it and point every AI tool at it before writing.

    1. 1
      Create a folder on a shared drive. Give it a clear, unique name like [Brand] - Brand Brain so anyone can find it. Use wherever your team already works - Google Drive or SharePoint.
    2. 2
      Open it and drop in the five files. Keep all five together: brand-profile, style-guide, audience, keyword-map, and never-do.
    3. 3
      Share the folder with your team. Give everyone who writes or prompts read access. One folder, one version - everyone starts from the same place.
    4. 4
      Point your AI at it. For a quick chat, paste or link the files first. For an agent, add the folder to its knowledge base with an instruction to always read it before responding.
    The one-line start prompt
    Before we begin, read the brand context in these files and follow it in everything you write: [paste or link the Brand Brain files].

    Keep it current. When something changes - a new rule, a new competitor - update the file in the shared folder. Every prompt and agent picks it up next time. No re-briefing.
